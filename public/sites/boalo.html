1 Public information

A single 70,484 m² holding of meadow and dehesa on the southern foothills of the Sierra de Guadarrama, at the edge of El Boalo — pine and granite outcrops, old stone walls, and the Torre del Parro on the horizon. Access from Calle Berrocal on the eastern boundary; the village core is minutes away and the site sits against the buffer of the Parque Nacional de Guadarrama's protected horizon.

2 What BYLD intends to do

A low-density wellness landscape estate: keep at least 75% of the land open as dehesa, and concentrate a phased program along one contour-following road — residences first, hospitality when the operating case is proven.
